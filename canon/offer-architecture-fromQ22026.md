# Product.Zone — Offer Architecture v2.0

## From Sprints to Journey Partnership

**Canonical Reference · March 2026**

---

## The Shift

**v1 (sprints):** We sell fixed interventions. Client has a problem, we run a sprint, we deliver, engagement ends. Revenue is project-based: €5K or €10K, then gone.

**v2 (journey partnership):** We design and maintain the client's complete buyer journey system. The canvas is the through-line. We fix what's broken now. We stay on the map. When the next stage breaks (and it will, because the business is growing), we're already in the conversation. Revenue compounds across the relationship.

**What stays the same:**

- Fixed scope at every tier. The founder knows what they're buying.
- Decisions first, surfaces second. The sequence is non-negotiable.
- Outside-in, buyer-question-led diagnostic. Surfaces don't lie.
- Handoff-ready output. The test: can someone else run this without the founder?

**What changes:**

- The canvas replaces the scorecard as the primary diagnostic and relationship tool.
- Products are defined by which journey stages they fix, not by methodology name.
- The relationship has a designed lifecycle: Map → Fix → Monitor → Extend.
- Partners plug into specific stages. PZ owns the map.

---

## The Relationship Lifecycle

```
MAP                     FIX                    MONITOR                 EXTEND
Canvas + Diagnostic     Sprint on broken       Quarterly review        Next bottleneck
                        stages                 + health check          emerges, fix it

€3–5K                   €8–15K                 €2K/quarter             €8–15K
1 week                  3–6 weeks              Ongoing                 3–6 weeks
Every client            Scoped by canvas       Stays on the map        Repeat as needed
```

The client never leaves the map. The canvas is updated at every stage. Each engagement deepens the system. The relationship compounds.

**Typical lifecycle:**

- Year 1: Map (€5K) + Fix (€12K) + 2 quarterly reviews (€4K) = €21K
- Year 2: 2 Fix interventions (€16K) + 4 quarterly reviews (€8K) = €24K
- Lifetime: €45K+ per client vs. €5–15K in the old model

## Product 0: Buyability Scan (Free)

### Purpose

Lead magnet and self-qualification. Founder self-selects their situation, enters their homepage URL, receives a situation-framed diagnostic. Creates awareness and routes to the Map engagement.

### Format

Self-serve. Automated via Hurozo. No session required.

### Output

- Buyability score (10 questions, situation-weighted)
- Top 3 gaps with situation-specific framing
- Verdict: Strong / Gaps Found / Weak
- CTA to full diagnostic

### What it does NOT show

- Decision inference
- Cross-surface analysis
- Action plan
- Full journey view

### Routing

- Gaps Found or Weak → "The full diagnostic covers your complete buyer journey. Book a call."
- Strong → "Your homepage works. The diagnostic checks the full journey including Find, Validate, and your sales motion."

## Product 1: The Map (Journey Diagnostic)

### Price

€3,000 – €5,000 (depending on surface count and complexity)

### Duration

1 week. Same structure as the current X-RAY, expanded to cover the full journey.

### Purpose

Map the client's complete buyer journey. Score each stage. Identify the primary bottleneck. Produce the canvas (Mode 2: evidence-based) and an action plan that scopes the Fix engagement.

### What is included

**Pre-session work (PZ team, 2–3 days):**

- Homepage audit: 20 buyer questions scored
- Find stage audit: LinkedIn posts, profile, ads (whichever exist) scored against Find questions
- Deck audit (if deck exists): 10 questions scored
- Outbound audit (if outbound exists): 5 questions scored
- Company context research

**Founder session (90 minutes):**

- Walk through the full journey canvas together
- Fill in what PZ can't see from outside: Activate, Embed, Expand, Advocate stages
- Confirm or challenge the stage scores
- Identify metrics that exist vs. unmeasured stages
- Prioritize: which stage to fix first?

**Post-session work (PZ team, 2 days):**

- Scored canvas (Mode 2) with evidence at each stage
- Decision audit: which of 31 decisions are locked, fuzzy, or missing
- Primary bottleneck identified with evidence trail
- Action plan: what to fix, in what order, estimated scope
- Offer recommendation: which Fix engagement matches the bottleneck

### Deliverables

1. **Buyer Journey Canvas** (scored, evidence-based, full 11 stages)
2. **Decision Ledger** (31 decisions, status + evidence)
3. **Surface Review** (every audited surface scored with buyer questions)
4. **Action Plan** (prioritized, scoped, with estimated Fix engagement)

### What is NOT included

- Any surface builds
- Any decision locking (that's the Fix)
- Ongoing monitoring (that's the Review)

### Entry criteria

- B2B software product with working product
- Founder-CEO available for 90-minute session + 30-minute walkthrough
- Homepage URL. Deck, outbound, and other surfaces optional but valuable.

### Exit state

Founder has a complete map of their buyer journey with evidence-based scoring at every stage. They know exactly where the path breaks and what to fix first. The Fix engagement is scoped and ready to start.

### The canvas as relationship anchor

The canvas created in The Map becomes the persistent reference for the entire relationship. Every future engagement updates it. Every quarterly review re-scores it. It's the shared operating system between PZ and the client.

## Product 2: The Fix (Modular Sprint)

### Price

€8,000 – €15,000 (depending on module count and complexity)

### Duration

3–6 weeks. Session-based, same rhythm as current sprints.

### Purpose

Fix the broken stages identified in The Map. The intervention is assembled from proven modules, scoped by the canvas.

### The Module System

Each module addresses a specific cluster of journey stages. Modules are combined based on what the canvas revealed.

| Module               | What it fixes                                                | Stages                        | Sessions | Typical scope                                                |
| -------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------- | -------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Positioning Lock** | Core positioning decisions (A1-A7) locked in buyer language  | Land, Make Sense, Self-Select | 2–3      | Category, customer, alternative, difference, outcome, wedge, market bet |
| **Surface Build**    | Key buyer-facing surfaces rebuilt from locked decisions      | Land → Commit                 | 2–3      | Homepage, deck, outbound templates                           |
| **Motion Install**   | Sales system built so others can sell                        | Compare → Commit              | 2–3      | Sales brief, objection map, demo script, handover docs       |
| **Find Design**      | Early journey designed: content strategy, audience architecture, channel plan | Find → Land                   | 2–3      | Content framework, audience segmentation, profile optimization, measurement |
| **Proof System**     | Evidence architecture that makes buying safe                 | Validate                      | 1–2      | Proof pack, case studies, champion deck, ROI framework       |
| **System Map**       | Full journey measurement framework designed                  | All stages                    | 1–2      | Input/output metrics per stage, dashboard design, review cadence |

### Common Combinations

| Client situation                          | Modules                                           | Price | Duration |
| ----------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- | ----- | -------- |
| **First sales stuck** (most common)       | Positioning Lock + Surface Build                  | €10K  | 4 weeks  |
| **Story won't travel**                    | Positioning Lock + Surface Build + Motion Install | €15K  | 6 weeks  |
| **Hiring into fog**                       | Positioning Lock + Motion Install                 | €10K  | 4 weeks  |
| **Early journey broken** (Pascal pattern) | Find Design + System Map                          | €8K   | 3 weeks  |
| **Post-Sprint, journey needs design**     | System Map + Find Design                          | €8K   | 3 weeks  |
| **Too many bets**                         | Positioning Lock (segment focus)                  | €8K   | 3 weeks  |
| **Proof gap**                             | Proof System + Surface Build (proof sections)     | €10K  | 4 weeks  |

### Pricing logic

- 1–2 modules: €8K
- 3 modules: €10K–12K
- 4 modules: €15K
- The current Entry Product (€5K) becomes the Positioning Lock module at €8K (with the canvas as the new entry, the module is no longer the first thing the client buys)
- The current Full Sprint (€10K) is roughly Positioning Lock + Surface Build + Motion Install at €15K (price increase justified by canvas + expanded scope)

### Principles (unchanged from v1)

- Sessions are decision-making and review moments, not working sessions
- All building work happens between sessions
- Founder arrives to find drafts ready, options visible, recommendation on table
- Fixed scope at every tier

### What is included per module

Each module produces:

- Locked decisions for that stage cluster
- Built surfaces for that stage cluster
- Updated canvas (the broken stages should move from red to yellow/green)
- Handover documentation

### Entry criteria

- The Map completed (canvas exists, bottleneck identified)
- Founder-CEO available for sessions
- For modules that build on Positioning Lock: positioning decisions must be locked first

### Exit state

The fixed stages move from red/yellow to green on the canvas. The founder has the surfaces and documentation for those stages. The canvas is updated to reflect the new state. The next bottleneck (if any) is visible.

## Product 3: The Review (Quarterly Health Check)

### Price

€2,000 per quarter (€8K/year if ongoing)

### Duration

Quarterly. One 90-minute session + re-scored canvas.

### Purpose

Re-score the canvas against the current state of surfaces. Identify drift. Catch problems before they compound. Plan the next Fix if needed.

### What is included

- Re-audit of all previously scored surfaces against buyer questions
- Updated canvas with current scores vs. previous scores
- Drift report: which decisions were locked and have become fuzzy again
- Recommendation: maintain, adjust, or fix
- 90-minute session to review findings and plan

### What is NOT included

- Surface rebuilds (that's a new Fix engagement)
- New surface creation
- Execution support

### The retention mechanism

The Review is how PZ stays in the relationship. The canvas is the thread. Every quarter, the founder sees: "These stages are still green. This one drifted to yellow. Find stage improved since the last review." The conversation is evidence-based, not opinion-based.

When a stage drifts to red, the next Fix engagement scopes itself naturally from the Review findings. No re-selling needed. The evidence is on the canvas.

### Entry criteria

- At least one Fix engagement completed
- Canvas exists from The Map

## Product 4: Partner Routing

### Not a product. An operating model.

PZ doesn't fix every stage. PZ owns the map and routes to the right partner for stages outside core capability.

| Stage cluster               | PZ capability                                  | Partner opportunity                                          |
| --------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Find (content, ads, social) | Find Design (strategy + architecture)          | Content agencies, ad agencies, LinkedIn specialists for execution |
| Land → Commit (buying path) | **Core.** Positioning, surfaces, motion.       | —                                                            |
| Activate, Embed             | Adjacent. Can advise on activation design.     | Product consultants, UX firms, onboarding specialists        |
| Expand                      | Outside core. Can identify expansion triggers. | Growth consultants, RevOps                                   |
| Advocate                    | Outside core. Can design referral asks.        | Community builders, referral program specialists             |

**The partner value proposition:** "PZ mapped the full journey and identified your bottleneck is at [stage]. Here's exactly what needs to happen. This partner specializes in that stage. We'll update the canvas when they're done and identify the next constraint."

PZ earns from the Map, the Fix (on core stages), and the Review. Partners earn from execution on their stages. The canvas connects everything.

**Alex (Profitbl) example:** After PZ completes Find Design (strategy, audience architecture, content framework), Profitbl executes the outbound motion. PZ designed it, Profitbl runs it. The Review checks whether it's working.

## The Sales Conversation Flow

**Before the call:** Run the free Buyability Scan on their homepage. Arrive with a point of view.

**On the call:** "I ran a quick scan on your homepage. Here's what I found. But the homepage is only one piece. The real question is: where does your full buyer journey break? That's what The Map shows you."

**The Map as entry:** "One week. We score your full journey from how buyers find you to how they commit. You get a canvas that shows exactly where the path breaks and what to fix first. €3-5K. The canvas is yours even if you don't proceed."

**After The Map:** "The canvas shows [stage X] is broken. Here's what's causing it: [evidence]. The Fix is a [N]-week sprint focused on those stages. [Price]. The rest of the journey is green. We don't touch what's working."

**After The Fix:** "The stages we fixed are green now. Here's the updated canvas. Want to set up a quarterly review so we catch drift early? €2K per quarter. I'll re-score everything and we'll stay ahead of problems."

The conversation never needs a hard sell. The evidence sells. The canvas creates the continuity.

## What Makes This Defensible

1. **The canvas is proprietary.** No other firm maps the full B2B buyer journey with buyer-question-level evidence at every stage. Agencies audit homepages. Consultants give opinions. PZ scores every stage with a formalized question set and traces problems to specific missing decisions.

2. **The diagnostic compounds.** Every engagement adds to the canvas. The client's history with PZ becomes increasingly valuable. Switching costs rise naturally because no one else has the baseline data.

3. **The partner model scales.** PZ doesn't need to build capability at every stage. PZ owns the map. Partners plug in. This model can scale to 50+ clients with a small core team because PZ only delivers on core stages (Land through Commit + Find Design + System Map).

4. **The lead magnet is the first step.** The free scan creates the conversation. The Map creates the relationship. The Fix creates the value. The Review creates the retention. Each step leads naturally to the next.
