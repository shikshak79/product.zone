## Design Principles

**Reputation first, reach second.**
The goal is not volume — it is to become the recognisable point of view for technical founder-CEOs navigating their first commercial transitions. When the right founder encounters Product.Zone, they should feel: "this person understands the exact problem I'm in."

**Signal-triggered outreach, not cold contact.**
Outbound only happens after a signal. A profile visit, a comment, a like, a referral introduction, a pre-qualification from a partner — these are the triggers. Direct cold outreach without signal is not consistent with how Product.Zone wants to be known, and not consistent with how the target buyer wants to be approached.

**Content is infrastructure, not activity.**
Every post, comment, and article is linked to the canonical frameworks (Revenue Loop, Buying Path X-RAY). Feedback from content goes back into the frameworks. The content engine is not separate from the methodology — it is the methodology made visible. Publishing from a stable foundation produces compounding signal. Publishing from an unstable foundation produces noise.

**The funnel is: reputation → warm signal → conversation → diagnosis → sale.**
Not: content → form fill → sales call. The path is longer and more human. It compounds.

**Current phase: install the engine, not run at full speed.**
The LinkedIn engine is being installed with Roelof Otten from April. The Emmanuel Mondon channel is being tested from late March. The agency routing channel is opening in parallel. This document defines the intended steady-state system. Current execution is building toward it.

## Channel Architecture

### Channel 1: LinkedIn Content Engine
**Status:** Installing (April, with Roelof Otten)
**Role:** Primary reputation and warm signal generator
**Model:** Justin Welsh / reputation-led model — consistent publishing builds a recognisable point of view, warms future buyers before contact

**How it works:**
- Consistent publishing schedule (cadence to be set with Roelof)
- Every post rooted in canonical frameworks (Revenue Loop, Buying Path X-RAY)
- Content categories: POV posts, tactical frameworks, diagnostic observations, founder journey recognition
- Feedback loop: content reactions → refine frameworks → stronger next post
- Metric: profile views, comments from target ICP, inbound DMs from qualified founders

**Signal types generated:**
- Profile visits from ICP founders
- Comments from founders recognising their situation
- Likes and shares that extend reach to new networks
- Connection requests from qualified prospects
- DMs opening with "I've been following your content"

**Outreach trigger:** Profile visit from an ICP-matching founder → warm connection request with specific observation ("I noticed you viewed my profile — are you working on [X]?")

**What changes once installed:**
Content engine removes the dependency on cold outreach and partner introductions as primary pipeline. Inbound signal becomes the dominant lead source. Outbound becomes signal-triggered and high-conversion rather than volume-dependent.

### Channel 2: Partner Pre-Qualification (Emmanuel Mondon)
**Status:** Active — 5-week test starting late March
**Role:** Qualified pipeline from the space/geo sector and adjacent networks
**Model:** Emmanuel scouts and pre-qualifies 2 startups per week; qualified prospects are routed to Product.Zone sales conversation

**How it works:**
- Emmanuel applies a pre-qualification filter based on agreed ICP criteria
- Pre-qualified founders introduced to Thorsten with context (situation summary, what Emmanuel observed)
- Thorsten runs standard sales process — call 1 is warm (context already set)
- Referral economics: apply at current price points; improve materially as pricing scales upward

**ICP filter for Emmanuel:**
- Post-product B2B software
- Founder-CEO available and engaged
- Named commercial transition (improvising sales, story not travelling, motion not scalable)
- Budget range consistent with entry product or full sprint

**Success criteria for 5-week test:**
- At least 2 qualified sales conversations generated
- At least 1 moves to proposal stage
- Feedback on ICP filter accuracy after each introduction

**Long-term potential:**
If the test validates the model, the arrangement scales to other partners with similar networks (VC scouts, accelerators, other agency-type partners). The "different door, same engine" principle applies — any partner who serves technical founder-CEOs and encounters GTM friction is a natural routing source.

### Channel 3: Agency Routing
**Status:** Opening
**Role:** Lead generation agencies encounter founders who are not ready for outbound execution; route them to Product.Zone to get positioned first
**Model:** Agency identifies a prospect who has a positioning/messaging problem before they can benefit from lead gen → refers to Product.Zone → founder does Entry Product or Sprint → returns to agency for execution

**Why this works:**
- Lead gen agencies lose deals when prospects are not ready ("your positioning isn't clear enough for us to run effective outbound")
- Product.Zone solves the pre-requisite problem
- Founder returns to the agency more valuable — the agency gets a better client
- Product.Zone gets a warm, pre-diagnosed referral

**How to activate:**
- Identify 2–3 lead gen agencies serving European B2B SaaS founders
- Frame the routing conversation: "When a prospect's positioning is unclear, you lose the deal. We fix the positioning. You get the client back."
- Agree informal routing arrangement — no formal contract required at this stage
- Track: how many referrals, how many convert, quality of the introductions

**Positioning for the agency:**
Product.Zone is not a competitor. Product.Zone is the prerequisite. Agencies who route founders to us get better clients back.

## Content Engine: Structure and Principles

### The Foundation Lock
All content is published from a stable foundation. The Revenue Loop and Buying Path X-RAY Architecture are the canonical references. Every post, comment, or article should be traceable to a decision, a zone, a stage, or a principle in those documents.

**This is why the content engine was paused.** Publishing before the foundation was stable produced noise. The foundation is now being locked through this operating model session. Content can resume at scale once the docs are committed to GitHub.

### Content Categories

**1. POV Posts**
Strong opinions on how B2B founders misunderstand their commercial situation. Rooted in framework principles. No hedging.
- Example: "Most founders think they have a sales problem. They have a buying path problem."
- Example: "The deck isn't the problem. The decisions the deck depends on are."
- Example: "Hiring a rep before your story travels is expensive validation of the wrong hypothesis."

**2. Diagnostic Observations**
Pattern-based observations from real diagnostic work (anonymised). Show the methodology in action.
- Example: "Audited a homepage this week. 2/20 buyer questions answered explicitly. Founder thought their positioning was clear. The surface told a different story."
- Example: "Q1, Q9, Q10 all missing on every surface. Classic PULL problem — not a copy problem. Rewriting the hero won't fix this."

**3. Tactical Frameworks**
Concrete, reusable frameworks a founder can apply immediately. High share value. Builds reputation as a thinking practitioner.
- Example: The 20 buyer questions a homepage must answer
- Example: The three commercial transitions every B2B founder hits
- Example: How to know if your pitch deck travels without you

**4. Founder Journey Recognition**
Posts that make the right founder feel seen. Based on the 8 Traps / commercial transitions framing.
- Example: "The moment you realise your story still needs you in the room to work."
- Example: "You're not bad at sales. You're trying to scale a motion that hasn't been translated yet."

**5. Proof and Results**
Client outcomes that prove the methodology works. Framed as evidence of a principle, not just a case study.
- Example: "Locked 18 positioning decisions in 3 sessions. Homepage went from 3/20 to 17/20 explicit buyer questions answered. Here's what changed."

### Posting Cadence (Target steady-state with Roelof)
- 3–4 original posts per week
- Daily commenting on ICP-adjacent content (signal generation without original content burden)
- Occasional long-form (newsletter or article) for high-signal deep dives

### The Feedback Loop
Content reactions are diagnostic signals:
- High engagement from ICP founders → this POV resonates, build more in this direction
- Comments from founders describing their situation → qualified warm signal, note for outreach
- No engagement → either wrong audience or wrong framing, diagnose before publishing more of the same
- 4000+ views on a single comment → this specific observation is resonating at scale, develop into a post

## Signal-Triggered Outreach Protocol

Product.Zone does not send cold outreach. Every outreach is triggered by a prior signal.

### Signal Hierarchy

| Signal | Strength | Response |
|---|---|---|
| Partner introduction (Emmanuel / agency) | Very strong | Direct sales conversation |
| DM initiated by prospect | Very strong | Respond with diagnostic curiosity |
| Comment on your post (ICP match) | Strong | Reply in thread, then connect with context |
| Profile visit from ICP founder | Medium | Connect with specific observation |
| Like on post (ICP match) | Medium | Connect with reference to the post |
| Comment on their content (cold) | Weak | Comment first, connect later |

### Outreach Messages

**After profile visit:**
> "I noticed you had a look at my profile — are you working through [commercial transition they seem to be in based on their profile]? Happy to share something useful if so."

**After comment on your post:**
> "Good observation in the thread — the [specific thing they said] maps to exactly the pattern I see in [related framework point]. Worth a conversation if you're working through that right now."

**After partner introduction:**
> "Emmanuel mentioned you're [situation summary]. That's exactly the kind of thing I work on. Happy to spend 20 minutes giving you a first read — no commitment."

### What not to send
- Generic connection requests without a specific observation
- Immediate pitch after connecting ("I help founders with GTM, here's my calendar")
- Follow-up sequences (more than one follow-up message without a response)

## The Product Ladder (Current vs. Intended)

### Current ladder (what drives marketing today)
```
FREE     X-RAY Diagnostic     Self-serve, routes to paid
€5K      Entry Product        Positioning & Messaging Sprint
€10K     Full Sprint          Buyability & Sales Motion Sprint
€2K/mo   Stabilisation        After Sprint retainer
```

Marketing drives toward: **X-RAY completion → Entry Product conversation.**

### Future ladder (phase 2 — not in current scope)
A mid-tier digital product layer (€50–€500 range) between the free X-RAY and the €5K Entry Product would reduce friction for founders who want to engage before committing to a sprint.

Possible formats:
- Self-serve decision ledger template (€50–€100)
- Messaging spine workbook (€100–€200)
- Recorded diagnostic workshop (€200–€500)

**This layer does not exist yet and is not in the current operating model.** It is a separate product design workstream that becomes relevant once the LinkedIn engine is producing consistent inbound signal. Build the audience first, then optimise the conversion ladder.
