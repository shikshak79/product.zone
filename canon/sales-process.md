# Product.Zone — Sales Process
**Canonical Reference · v1.0 · March 2026**
*Aligned to Buying Path X-RAY Architecture v5.1*

---

## Design Principles

**Point of view earns the right to present.**
The sales call does not open with a pitch. It opens with short discovery to confirm scope. Once scope is confirmed and the founder signals readiness, the deck enters as a structured point of view — not a proposal.

**Qualification happens through questions, not forms.**
Two questions determine whether to proceed: where is this founder in their journey, and what is the project they are trying to move right now? If neither answer is clear, the call is not qualified.

**The deck is a diagnostic tool, not a brochure.**
The pitch deck enters only after 2–3 qualifying questions land well. It is used to structure the founder's recognition of their own situation — not to explain Product.Zone's features.

**Close with a specific next step, not an open door.**
Every call that goes well ends with a named decision point and a timeline. "Let me know when you're ready" is not a close. "Does it make sense to decide by tomorrow evening?" is.

## Call Flow Overview

```
PRE-CALL
  Intake materials reviewed (if available)
  Surface scan completed
  ↓
OPEN — Short Discovery (5–10 min)
  Confirm scope, stage, current situation
  ↓
SCOPE CHECK (2 min)
  Internal read: are we in scope?
  If yes → continue
  If no → redirect honestly
  ↓
PERMISSION (1 min)
  "I have a few slides that might be useful —
   want to take a look?"
  ↓
POINT OF VIEW — Deck (15–20 min)
  Present 2–3 qualifying questions first
  Pull out deck after signals are clear
  Walk through relevant sections
  ↓
DIAGNOSIS (10 min)
  Reflect situation back
  Name the transition they're stuck in
  Connect to the right product
  ↓
COMMERCIAL (5 min)
  Present the relevant offer(s)
  Entry Product vs. Full Sprint based on diagnosis
  ↓
CLOSE (5 min)
  Agree explicit next step + timeline
  Send condensed deck link (Figma)
  ↓
POST-CALL (same day)
  Follow-up message sent
  Next step confirmed in writing
```

## Phase 1: Open — Short Discovery

**Purpose:** Confirm scope before investing time. Check that the founder's situation and Product.Zone's methodology are a genuine match. This is not full discovery — it is a fast filter.

**Duration:** 5–10 minutes

**Opening move:**
Do not open with a product pitch. Open with a situation question that invites the founder to place themselves.

> *"Before we get into anything — where are you right now? What's the current state of things commercially?"*

This question does two things simultaneously:
- Reveals the founder's stage in the journey (pre-first-sale, inconsistent traction, trying to scale beyond founder)
- Surfaces the emotional texture — frustrated, confused, urgent, exploratory

**Listen for:**
- Which of the three commercial transitions this founder is in
- Whether they have a named problem or a vague discomfort
- Whether urgency is real or theoretical

---

## Phase 2: The Two Qualifying Questions

These are the two questions that determine whether to proceed. Neither is skippable.

### Question 1: Stage

> *"Where are you in the founder journey — first sales, trying to bring others in, or trying to get it out of your head entirely?"*

**What this unlocks:** Confirms which transition they're in and which product is relevant.

**Strong signal:**
- Founder can name their situation precisely
- There is a specific recent event that crystallised the problem ("we just lost our second deal in a row for the same reason")
- The stage maps cleanly to one of the three transitions

**Weak signal:**
- "We're doing okay but want to improve"
- Vague optimism with no named problem
- No recent forcing event

**PULL connection:** This question surfaces U (Unavoidable) — is this a real priority right now, or theoretical?

---

### Question 2: The Project

> *"What's the one important project you're working on right now — the thing you absolutely need to move?"*

**What this unlocks:** The PULL diagnostic P signal — whether the founder has a specific project underway that Product.Zone's methodology directly serves. This is the most important qualifying question in the entire call.

**Strong signal (in scope):**
- "We're rewriting our homepage because deals keep stalling"
- "I need to onboard a sales rep and I don't know how to brief them"
- "We keep losing to [competitor] and I can't explain why we're better"
- "My pitch is different every time and I need to fix that"

**Weak signal (may be out of scope):**
- "We need more leads" (demand generation problem, not positioning)
- "We need to close faster" (process problem, not buying path)
- "We need better CRM" (RevOps, not methodology)
- "I want to scale" (vague ambition, no specific project)

**PULL connection:** This question surfaces P (Project) — the specific task on their list — and begins to reveal L (Look) and L (Lacking) through their description of what they've already tried.

## Phase 3: Scope Check

After the two qualifying questions, make an internal read before proceeding.

### In Scope

Proceed with deck if all three are true:
- Founder is in a named commercial transition (not "we're fine, just exploring")
- There is a specific project underway that maps to positioning, buying path, or sales motion work
- The problem is about message, story, or system — not pure lead volume or tactical execution

### Out of Scope

Redirect honestly if any of these are true:
- Problem is primarily demand generation ("we need more leads, our positioning is fine")
- Product is pre-revenue or pre-product (no surfaces to diagnose, no sales pattern to fix)
- Problem is operational/RevOps (CRM, pipeline process, territory design)
- Founder is looking for a coach or thinking partner, not a delivery engagement

**How to redirect:**
> *"Based on what you're describing, I think the most useful thing I can do right now is point you toward [X] — that's closer to what you actually need. What I do is specifically about [positioning, buying path, sales motion] — want me to explain the distinction so you can decide if it's relevant?"*

Redirecting cleanly is a trust signal, not a failure. The founder remembers it.

---

## Phase 4: Permission and Deck Entry

Once scope is confirmed, ask permission before pulling out the deck.

> *"I've got a few slides that might put a shape on what you're describing. Want to take a look?"*

This does three things:
- Creates a micro-commitment (they said yes)
- Signals that the deck is contextual, not a standard pitch
- Maintains conversational rhythm

**When the deck enters:**
- After 2–3 qualifying questions land clearly — not before
- When the founder has named a specific situation that the deck speaks to
- Sometimes this is late in call 1; sometimes the deck leads call 2 if call 1 was exploratory

**Which sections to present:**
Adapt based on the founder's named situation. Not every slide every time.

| Founder situation | Primary slides to present |
|---|---|
| "My pitch is inconsistent" | Founder journey → Hidden cause → System slide |
| "My rep can't carry the story" | Hidden cause → What founders try → System slide |
| "Buyers don't get it" | Founder journey → Hidden cause → Proof |
| "I need to scale beyond me" | Perfect world → System slide → How we work |
| "We sound like everyone else" | What founders try → System slide → Who this is for |

---

## Phase 5: Diagnosis

After the deck, reflect the founder's situation back before naming a product.

**Do not jump straight to "here's what I'd recommend."**

First, show you understood:

> *"Based on what you've described — [their situation in their words] — it sounds like you're stuck at [transition name]. The pattern I see is [specific gap]. Does that land?"*

Then name the constraint in framework terms:

> *"What's making this hard is that [the buying path is still in your head / the story doesn't survive without you / the surfaces are saying different things]. That's what the [Entry Product / Sprint] is specifically designed to fix."*

**This is the moment the founder feels diagnosed, not pitched.**

If they push back or add nuance, update the read. Don't defend the diagnosis — sharpen it.

---

## Phase 6: Commercial

Present the relevant product tier based on the diagnosis. Not all tiers. The relevant one.

**Routing logic:**

| Diagnosis | Recommended entry |
|---|---|
| Decisions fuzzy, no surfaces built | Entry Product (€5K, 3 weeks) |
| Decisions partially locked, surfaces inconsistent | Entry Product |
| Decisions locked, motion not installed | Full Sprint (€10K, 6 weeks) |
| X-RAY already completed, gaps clear | Full Sprint direct |

**How to present:**

> *"Given where you are, the right starting point is [Entry Product / Full Sprint]. Here's what it covers and what you leave with..."*

Walk through:
- Scope (what's included, what's not)
- Duration (standard vs. fast track option)
- What they leave with (the exit state — decisions locked / motion installed)
- Price

**Do not present all tiers simultaneously.** Present the one that fits. If they ask about the other, explain the routing logic. This keeps the conversation diagnostic, not a menu selection.

**On the Full Sprint upsell:**
If presenting Entry Product, do not pitch the Full Sprint in the same breath. The entry product is sold on its own merits. The Sprint conversation happens naturally at the handover session once they've seen the value. Mentioning it here is premature and creates confusion.

---

## Phase 7: Close

**Every call that goes well ends with a named next step and a timeline.**

**Standard close:**

> *"Does this feel like the right fit for where you are? If so, does it make sense to decide by [tomorrow evening / end of week]? I can send you the deck link now so you have it in front of you."*

**What to send immediately after the call:**
- Condensed deck as Figma presentation link (trackable — not PDF attachment)
- 2–3 sentence summary of what you heard and what you recommended
- The explicit next step and timeline agreed on the call

**Example follow-up message:**

> *"Good to talk. Based on what you described — [1 sentence summary of their situation] — the [Entry Product / Full Sprint] is the right starting point. Here's the deck for reference: [Figma link]. Let me know by [day] if you want to proceed and I'll send the agreement."*

**If they ask for more time:**

> *"Of course — what would you need to see or know to feel confident about the decision?"*

This surfaces the real objection. Answer it directly, then reset the timeline.

## Qualification Criteria Summary

### Strong fit (proceed)

- Post-product, B2B software, at least one paying customer or active pilot
- Founder-CEO is the decision-maker and will be in every session
- Named commercial transition: first sales / bringing in others / scaling beyond founder
- Specific project underway that maps to positioning, buying path, or motion work
- Budget ownership clear (founder controls spend decision)
- Available for 3 or 6 weekly sessions

### Weak fit (explore before proceeding)

- Problem described as "we need more leads" without a positioning or story problem underneath
- Founder is delegating the engagement to a team member (not in sessions themselves)
- No clear project — vague desire to "improve GTM"
- Very early stage (pre-revenue, pre-product) — no surfaces to audit, no sales pattern to diagnose

### Out of scope (redirect)

- Pre-product or idea stage
- Primary problem is demand generation or paid media
- Enterprise sales with complex procurement (wrong methodology)
- Looking for ongoing advisory retainer without a defined project (Estner model, not Product.Zone)
- Budget clearly below entry product price without a path to approval

## Post-Call Protocol

### Same day

- [ ] Send follow-up message with Figma deck link
- [ ] Confirm next step and timeline in writing
- [ ] Log call in CRM: stage, situation summary, product recommended, next step, timeline

### Within 48 hours

- [ ] If no response: one follow-up nudge ("Just checking you got the link — happy to answer any questions")
- [ ] If decision pending: honour the timeline, don't chase before it

### If they go quiet

After agreed timeline passes without response:

> *"I know these decisions take time. If now isn't the right moment, no problem — I'd rather you reach out when it makes sense than rush into something. Happy to pick up whenever."*

One message. Then let it rest. No chase sequence.

## Call 2 Protocol

Some calls require a second conversation — typically when call 1 was exploratory and more context was needed before presenting the deck.

**When to suggest call 2:**
- Call 1 was productive but scope was unclear — agree to "come back with a sharper read"
- Founder mentioned a specific surface issue (homepage, deck) and you want to review it before call 2
- Founder needs to loop in a co-founder or partner before deciding

**Between calls:**
- Review any surfaces shared
- Prepare a brief diagnostic read to open call 2 ("based on what you shared, here's what I'm seeing")
- This is the pre-work pattern from the engagement playbook — applied to sales

**Call 2 opens with:**
> *"I had a look at [homepage/deck] — here's what I noticed..."*

Starting call 2 with a specific observation signals that you've done the work. This alone converts more often than any pitch technique.

## The PULL Signal in Sales

The two qualifying questions map directly to the PULL diagnostic framework:

| PULL Element | Sales Question | What It Reveals |
|---|---|---|
| **P — Project** | "What's the one important project you're working on right now?" | Is there a specific task they're trying to accomplish? |
| **U — Unavoidable** | "Where are you in the founder journey?" + urgency probes | Is this genuinely their priority right now? |
| **L — Look** | "What have you tried so far?" | What options are they already considering? |
| **L — Lacking** | "What's made that hard?" | Why those options are inadequate |

When all four PULL elements are present and specific, the founder is ready to buy. When Project is vague or Unavoidable is theoretical, the timing is wrong — and no amount of deck quality fixes that.

**Demand intensity check:**
After the two qualifying questions, listen for the emotional register:
- "Hell yes, where have you been?" → proceed immediately
- "I can see how this would be useful" → PULL is not locked, probe deeper before proceeding
