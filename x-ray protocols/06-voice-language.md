# X-RAY Protocol: 06 — Voice & Language
## v5.3

---

## Overview

All X-RAY report content must follow Product.Zone voice standards. This protocol adapts the core voice reference for diagnostic report writing.

---

## The Standard

**Primary benchmark:** Matt Lakajev — direct, economically punchy, names the problem without diplomatic packaging.

**Secondary benchmark:** Emma Stratton — specific over vague, accessible, no jargon.

**The combination:** Direct, credible, useful to a founder trying to solve a real problem. Not corporate. Not therapy. Not motivational.

---

## X-RAY-Specific Voice Rules

### Diagnosis Headlines

Headlines should name the finding directly. No diplomatic softening.

**Wrong:** "There may be some opportunities to improve the homepage experience"  
**Right:** "Pain resonates, but buyers cannot place LabV, justify the switch, or take a safe first step"

### Evidence Inferences

Inferences should be specific and grounded, not vague observations.

**Wrong:** "This could potentially cause some confusion for visitors"  
**Right:** "Buyers cannot file this in a known category. Sales must explain from scratch."

### Recognition Signals (Expected Objections)

These should sound like real buyer quotes — direct, skeptical, economic.

**Wrong:** "I wonder if this solution might be appropriate for our organization"  
**Right:** "We already have a data management system. Why would we switch?"

### Recommendations

Actions should be specific and concrete, not abstract advice.

**Wrong:** "Consider enhancing the value proposition clarity"  
**Right:** "Name LIMS explicitly. 'Unlike traditional LIMS...' or 'If you've outgrown Excel but LIMS feels like overkill...'"

---

## AI Tells to Eliminate

The following patterns must be removed from all X-RAY report content:

### Negative Parallelisms
The most prominent AI tell. Cut all "It's not X, it's Y" constructions.

**Cut:** "It's not a messaging problem. It's a structure problem."  
**Keep:** "The messaging is fine. The buying path is broken."

### Hollow Transitions
Remove all:
- "It's worth noting that..."
- "It's important to understand that..."
- "This highlights the fact that..."
- "Notably..."
- "Interestingly..."

Start with the thing itself.

### Vague Attribution
Remove all:
- "Many buyers might..."
- "Research suggests..."
- "Some prospects may..."

Replace with specific observations from the homepage evidence.

### Inflated Verb Constructions
- "serves as" → is
- "functions as" → is
- "leverages" → uses
- "utilizes" → uses

### Promotional Inflation
Never describe findings as "fascinating", "remarkable", "significant", "crucial", or "critical" without evidence. State the finding plainly.

### Em Dashes
Never use em dashes. Replace with periods, commas, or colons.

### Excessive Bolding
Bold nothing in prose sections. If emphasis is needed, restructure the sentence.

---

## ESL Accessibility Rules

The reader may be a European founder with English as a second language.

**Rule 1:** No US-specific idioms. Replace "ballpark", "punt", "table" with plain equivalents.

**Rule 2:** No insider terminology without explanation. If a term needs a separate search, replace it.

**Rule 3:** Test: Could a German, Dutch, or French founder with solid but non-native English read this without stopping?

## Section-Specific Guidelines

### Diagnosis Section

| Element | Voice Rule |
|---------|------------|
| Verdict | Direct action statement. "Fix Before Outbound" not "May need some improvements" |
| Headline | Names specific failures. 20-40 words. No hedging. |
| Text | 2-3 sentences. Explains what will happen if outbound starts. Economic framing. |

### Predictions Section

| Element | Voice Rule |
|---------|------------|
| Metric values | Strong / Moderate / Weak — no hedging |
| Summary | One sentence. States consequence in economic terms. |

### Risk Signals

| Element | Voice Rule |
|---------|------------|
| Evidence | One sentence. Specific observation + buyer consequence. |

### Recognition Signals

| Element | Voice Rule |
|---------|------------|
| Quotes | Direct buyer voice. Skeptical. Economic. First-person. |

### Recommendations

| Element | Voice Rule |
|---------|------------|
| Labels | Verb-noun format. "Name the Alternative" not "Alternative Clarity Enhancement" |
| Descriptions | Specific action + example. No abstract advice. |

---

## Pre-Output Checklist

Before generating any X-RAY report content, verify:

- [ ] No negative parallelisms anywhere
- [ ] No hollow transitions
- [ ] No vague attributions
- [ ] No em dashes
- [ ] No promotional inflation
- [ ] All recommendations include specific examples
- [ ] Recognition signals sound like real buyer skepticism
- [ ] ESL-accessible (no US-specific idioms)
